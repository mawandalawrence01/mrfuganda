Skills & Leadership Development Pathways (SLDP)
The Skills & Leadership Development Pathways (SLDP) at Masaka Relief Foundation empowers youth and women to build sustainable livelihoods while nurturing the next generation of confident, capable leaders.
Our Focus
SLDP operates through two integrated pathways:
1. Economic Empowerment Pathway
We equip out-of-school youth and vulnerable women with practical, marketable skills that open doors to employment and entrepreneurship. Through vocational training, entrepreneurship development, mentorship, and job readiness programs, we create clear pathways toward income generation and financial independence.
Key interventions include:
•	Vocational and technical skills training
•	Entrepreneurship and financial literacy development
•	Mentorship and access to small business support
•	Employment readiness and career guidance
2. Leadership & Life Skills Pathway
We strengthen school-based clubs that foster leadership, critical thinking, and personal growth among in-school children and youth. Through interactive, student-led platforms, young people develop confidence, communication skills, and informed decision-making abilities.
Our school club initiatives focus on:
•	Debate and public speaking
•	Leadership and life skills development
•	Entrepreneurship and innovation
•	Sexual and reproductive health awareness
Our Approach
We believe economic empowerment and leadership development are interconnected. When youth and women gain skills, confidence, and access to opportunity, they become active contributors to their communities and drivers of sustainable change.
By partnering with schools, community groups, and local organizations, we ensure our programs are practical, inclusive, and responsive to real community needs.
Our Impact
Through SLDP, we aim to:
•	Increase employment and self-employment opportunities
•	Strengthen leadership capacity among young people
•	Improve household incomes and reduce vulnerability
•	Promote social inclusion and community resilience
SLDP is building clear pathways toward economic inclusion, leadership growth, and lasting community transformation.

Water, Sanitation & Hygiene (WASH) Pathway
The Water, Sanitation & Hygiene (WASH) Pathway at Masaka Relief Foundation strengthens access to safe water, improved sanitation, and healthy hygiene practices for vulnerable communities.
Building on our work in Greater Masaka, this pathway combines infrastructure development, sustainability measures, hygiene education, and community capacity building to ensure long-term health and water security.
Our Focus
We work across four integrated areas:
1. Water & Sanitation Infrastructure
We construct boreholes and provide water tanks to expand access to clean and safe water. We also support the construction of household and community latrines, improve drainage systems, and promote effective waste management to reduce disease and environmental contamination.
2. Sustainable Water Management
We conduct regular maintenance of boreholes and water tanks, establish community water user committees, and introduce rainwater harvesting and groundwater conservation initiatives to ensure reliable, climate-resilient water supply.
3. Hygiene Education & Behavior Change
Through community-led campaigns and workshops, we promote handwashing, safe water storage, and proper sanitation practices to reduce waterborne illnesses and improve overall public health.
4. Community Capacity & Economic Inclusion
We train local women and youth in WASH facility maintenance and related enterprises—such as water system management and hygiene product production—creating sustainability and livelihood opportunities.
Our Impact
Through the WASH Pathway, we aim to:
•	Reduce preventable waterborne diseases
•	Increase access to safe water and improved sanitation
•	Strengthen community ownership of WASH infrastructure
•	Build climate-resilient and healthier communities
The WASH Pathway ensures that vulnerable communities not only access clean water—but sustain it, protect it, and thrive because of it.
 



Child Protection & Family Strengthening Pathway
The Child Protection & Family Strengthening Pathway at Masaka Relief Foundation supports orphans and vulnerable children (OVCs) to grow, learn, and thrive in safe and stable environments.
We take a holistic approach that strengthens both children and their households—addressing education, health, nutrition, psychosocial well-being, and economic stability to break cycles of poverty and vulnerability.
Our Focus
1. Education Access & Advancement
We identify and enroll vulnerable children in school and provide scholarships, learning materials, mentorship, and tutoring to support academic success and long-term opportunity.
2. Household Economic Empowerment
We strengthen families by equipping caregivers with livelihood skills, small business support, and savings opportunities to improve household income and stability.
3. Health, Nutrition & Well-being
We promote child health through school feeding initiatives, health screenings, hygiene support, and referrals to essential services.
4. Community Child Protection
We work with community leaders, schools, and families to raise awareness on child rights, prevent abuse and exploitation, and build stronger local child protection systems.
Our Impact
Through this pathway, we aim to:
•	Increase school enrollment and retention for vulnerable children
•	Improve household income and family stability
•	Strengthen child protection systems at community level
•	Create safer, healthier, and more hopeful futures for children
The Child Protection & Family Strengthening Pathway ensures that every vulnerable child is protected, supported, and given the opportunity to reach their full potential.


Health Promotion Pathway
The Health Promotion Pathway at Masaka Relief Foundation advances community well-being by strengthening preventive health systems and expanding access to essential reproductive and mental health services.
We focus on empowering adolescents, women, and vulnerable populations with the knowledge, services, and support needed to make informed health decisions and live healthier, more resilient lives.
Our Focus
1. Reproductive Health & HIV Services
We improve public health outcomes by ensuring access to essential reproductive health services, including HIV/AIDS counseling and testing, referrals, and community awareness initiatives.
2. Mental Health & Psychosocial Support
We promote mental health awareness, early identification of emotional and behavioral challenges, and referrals for appropriate care. Through community dialogues and school-based initiatives, we reduce stigma and encourage open conversations about mental well-being.
3. Behavioral Change Communication
We implement targeted campaigns that promote positive health behaviors, reduce stigma, and encourage informed decision-making around sexual and reproductive health.
4. Hygiene & Disease Prevention
We support community-based handwashing promotion and preventive health practices that reduce the spread of communicable diseases.
5. School-Based Health Promotion
We strengthen menstrual health and hygiene programs in schools and support peer health clubs that provide safe spaces for learning, dialogue, leadership, and health advocacy.
Our Impact
Through the Health Promotion Pathway, we aim to:
•	Increase uptake of HIV testing and reproductive health services
•	Improve mental health awareness and access to psychosocial support
•	Strengthen hygiene behaviors and disease prevention practices
•	Reduce stigma surrounding sexual and mental health
•	Empower young people as health ambassadors in their schools and communities
The Health Promotion Pathway builds informed, healthy, and emotionally resilient communities through prevention, education, and access to essential health services.


Environmental Sustainability & Community Relief Pathway
The Environmental Sustainability & Community Relief Pathway at Masaka Relief Foundation responds to immediate household needs while promoting long-term environmental responsibility and resilience.
We combine humanitarian support with environmental action to ensure vulnerable families are not only supported in times of need but also empowered to build sustainable futures.
Our Focus
1. Food & Household Relief
We provide food aid and essential household support to vulnerable families facing hardship. During festival and special community days, we celebrate with families—restoring dignity, hope, and a sense of belonging.
2. Community Solidarity & Social Inclusion
We organize community outreach and shared celebrations that strengthen social bonds and ensure that marginalized families feel valued and supported.
3. Environmental Education
We conduct community sensitization programs on environmental conservation, climate awareness, and sustainable practices that protect natural resources for future generations.
4. Tree Planting & Climate Action
We promote tree planting initiatives and community-based environmental restoration projects that contribute to climate resilience, improved livelihoods, and healthier ecosystems.
Our Impact
Through this pathway, we aim to:
•	Reduce immediate hunger and vulnerability among at-risk families
•	Strengthen community solidarity and social inclusion
•	Promote environmental responsibility and climate resilience
•	Encourage sustainable practices that protect local ecosystems
The Environmental Sustainability & Community Relief Pathway ensures that while we respond to urgent needs today, we also protect and sustain the future for tomorrow.
